    Heat danger is not just about air temperature. As humidity increases, sweat evaporates more slowly, and your body loses one of its most important cooling mechanisms.

    This means the heat index can climb far above the raw temperature reading, especially in dense urban spaces.

    High humidity prevents the body from cooling down efficiently.

    Body Cooling Mechanisms

    How Does Our Body Cool Itself?

    The body relies on sweating and increased blood flow to the skin to release heat.

    SW

    Sweating

    Sweat evaporates from the skin and carries heat away.

    VD

    Vasodilation

    Blood vessels widen so more heat can be released through the skin.

    CF

    When cooling fails

    Heat stroke can occur when the body absorbs more heat than it can release.

    Heat stroke happens when heat gain exceeds heat loss.

    Heat Illness Basics

    What is Heat-Related Illness?

    HC

    Heat cramps

    Painful muscle cramps triggered by heat and heavy sweating.

    • Muscle spasms in legs or abdomen
    • Heavy sweating
    • Thirst and fatigue
    HE

    Heat exhaustion

    The body is struggling to cool itself, often after prolonged heat exposure.

    • Dizziness and weakness
    • Nausea or headache
    • Cool, clammy skin
    HS

    Heat stroke

    A medical emergency where body temperature can rise rapidly to life-threatening levels.

    • Confusion or loss of consciousness
    • Hot, dry skin or no sweat
    • Rapid pulse and high body temperature

    Prevention and Action

    How to Protect Yourself

    01

    Drink more water

    Hydrate steadily across the day, not only when thirsty.

    02

    Avoid peak heat hours

    Reduce outdoor activity during the hottest afternoon period.

    03

    Check heat warnings

    Use weather alerts to plan safer work and travel schedules.

    04

    Wear light clothing

    Choose breathable fabrics and lighter colors when possible.

    05

    Stay in cool environments

    Take frequent cooling breaks in shade or air-conditioned spaces.
